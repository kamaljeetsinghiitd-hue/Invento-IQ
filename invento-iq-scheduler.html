📐 AI Scheduling Rules

The 4 rules the AI follows to build the optimal schedule

1
Material Availability
Never schedule a product if its raw material is unavailable or will run out mid-shift. AI checks inventory before assigning.
Product A needs Steel →
Steel runs out Wed →
∴ Switch Wed to Product B/C
∴ Product A resumes Thu
2
Line Compatibility
Each product can only run on specific lines. AI never assigns a product to an incompatible line.
Line 1 → Product A ✅, B ✅, C ❌
Line 2 → Product A ❌, B ✅, C ✅
∴ Product A never on Line 2
∴ Product C never on Line 1
3
Minimise Changeovers
Every time a line switches products, it wastes 2–4 hours on cleaning and setup. AI groups same products together.
Bad: A→C→A→B→A (5 switches)
Good: A→A→A→B→C (2 switches)
Saving: 6–12 hrs/week
= 180–360 extra units/week!
4
Demand Priority
High priority orders are scheduled first. AI uses priority tiers to ensure critical orders ship on time.
P1: Urgent orders (today deadline)
P2: This week forecast demand
P3: Safety stock build-up
P4: Next week pre-production

Why Line 1 can't do Product C?

Why Line 2 can't do Product A?
Product A requires high-pressure steel forming at 800°C. Line 2's furnace only reaches 600°C. Running Product A on Line 2 would result in quality failure.
🧠 AI Insight — Product B is Strategic

Product B can run on both lines — making it the most flexible product. AI uses Product B as a "buffer" product to fill gaps when other products are blocked by material shortages or maintenance windows. This is why you see Product B appear on both lines across the week.
